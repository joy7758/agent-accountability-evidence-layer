# Reviewer Attack-Defense Matrix

This matrix is written for paper review. It records where an objection is
partially valid, how the paper should defend the contribution, which citation
keys and repository evidence support the answer, and what future work should do.

| # | attack | why it is partially valid | defense | citation_keys | repository_evidence | limitation_sentence | next_strengthening_step |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | This is just another JSON schema. | A schema alone would not be enough. | ASIEP includes schema, state machine, invariants, validator JSON, repair plans, bundle resolution, importer, packager, evaluator, paper linter, and citation linter. | `[@w3c_prov_dm; @slsa_provenance_v1_1]` | `schemas/asiep.schema.json`, `src/asiep_validator/`, `src/asiep_repairer/`, `src/asiep_resolver/`, `src/asiep_evaluator/` | The local implementation is still v0.1 and fixture-based. | Add independent implementation and external trace exports. |
| 2 | AgentDevel already solves this. | AgentDevel is a close related work on self-evolving agents and release engineering. | ASIEP targets the evidence object and verification layer around a self-improvement event, not the release-engineering method itself. | `[@agentdevel_2026; @self_refine_2023; @reflexion_2023; @voyager_2023]` | `manuscript/related_work_matrix.md`, `docs/research_note_v0.1.md`, `manuscript/claims_registry.json` | ASIEP should be framed as complementary, not superior. | Build a trace export from an AgentDevel-like workflow if data becomes available. |
| 3 | OpenTelemetry already records traces. | OTel is the right substrate for observability and includes GenAI/agent span conventions. | ASIEP treats OTel-like traces as source material and adds gate evidence, digest binding, repair plans, package manifests, and claim accountability. | `[@otel_genai_semconv; @otel_genai_agent_spans]` | `interfaces/otel_genai_trace_fixture.schema.json`, `src/asiep_importer/`, `evaluation/crosswalk/asiep_v0.1_crosswalk.json` | ASIEP does not integrate a real OTel collector in v0.1. | Implement a real local export adapter after the profile stabilizes. |
| 4 | W3C PROV already provides provenance. | PROV supplies the core entity, activity, and agent model. | ASIEP maps to PROV but adds ASIEP-specific invariants, gate decisions, repairability, bundle digest checks, and local package closure. | `[@w3c_prov_dm; @w3c_prov_o]` | `mappings/prov_mapping.md`, `interfaces/asiep_prov_jsonld.schema.json`, `evaluation/crosswalk/asiep_v0.1_crosswalk.json` | ASIEP does not claim complete PROV-O reasoning. | Add round-trip PROV validation in a later milestone. |
| 5 | RO-Crate/FDO already package evidence. | RO-Crate and FDO address packaging and machine-actionable objects. | ASIEP uses local RO-Crate-like and FDO-like outputs for exchange, while the ASIEP-specific contribution is the self-improvement evidence profile and validation path. | `[@workflow_run_rocrate; @process_run_crate; @fdo_architecture_spec]` | `src/asiep_packager/`, `interfaces/asiep_package_manifest.schema.json`, `interfaces/asiep_fdo_record.schema.json`, `interfaces/asiep_rocrate_metadata.schema.json` | M5 is local-like packaging, not external certification or registry submission. | Test with community RO-Crate tooling and real FDO infrastructure later. |
| 6 | Evaluation uses only local fixtures. | This is true and limits external validity. | The M6 claim is reproducibility over a controlled corpus, not deployment-scale performance. | `[@eu_ai_act_article_12; @nist_ai_agent_standards]` | `evaluation/corpus/asiep_v0.1_corpus.json`, `reports/asiep_v0.1_evaluation_report.json`, `paper_assets/tables/evaluation_metrics.md` | Metrics should be read as local conformance checks. | Add real trace exports and independently authored attack cases. |
| 7 | Metrics are too optimistic. | Small synthetic corpora can produce high values. | The paper reports numerator, denominator, method, and limitations for each metric and avoids benchmark framing. | `[@owasp_llm_top10]` | `interfaces/asiep_evaluation_report.schema.json`, `reports/asiep_v0.1_evaluation_report.json`, `docs/evaluation_method.md` | The metrics are not open-world adversarial robustness results. | Increase corpus size and add blind held-out invalid cases. |
| 8 | Privacy compliance is not real DLP. | Current checks are sentinel and key scans. | The paper says privacy compliance is local policy conformance, not full DLP or production privacy assurance. | `[@owasp_llm_top10]` | `src/asiep_importer/importer.py`, `src/asiep_evaluator/evaluator.py`, `manuscript/limitations.md` | Raw sensitive-content prevention is partial and fixture-scoped. | Add structured redaction policies and external DLP review in future work. |
| 9 | No real OTel / LangSmith integration. | Correct for M4. | The importer intentionally handles local fixtures to prove trace-to-evidence transformation without network or product dependency. | `[@otel_genai_semconv; @otel_genai_agent_spans; @langsmith_observability_concepts; @langsmith_feedback_docs]` | `examples/fixtures/`, `examples/import_requests/`, `scripts/import_trace_demo.py` | OTel and LangSmith are fixture shapes only in v0.1. | Add importers for exported traces, not live API calls, in a later milestone. |
| 10 | No real FDO registry or PID. | Correct for M5. | The package explicitly uses local identifiers and forbids fake PID or registry claims. | `[@fdo_architecture_spec]` | `profiles/asiep/v0.1/package_policy.json`, `src/asiep_packager/packager.py`, `docs/fdo_rocrate_packaging.md` | Package identifiers are local and not globally resolvable. | Explore registry submission only after package semantics are reviewed. |
| 11 | Repairer may encourage evidence tampering. | A repair loop can be dangerous if it silently edits evidence. | ASIEP repair plans are evidence-preserving: they can propose reject decisions or skeletons, but require real external evidence for gate reports, approvals, refs, and digests. | `[@slsa_provenance_v1_1; @owasp_llm_top10]` | `profiles/asiep/v0.1/repair_policy.json`, `src/asiep_repairer/`, `docs/repair_loop.md` | The repairer does not guarantee that a human or agent will follow policy outside this repository. | Add signed review steps and immutable repair audit records. |
| 12 | ASIEP may be too narrow because it focuses on self-improvement. | The profile does not cover all agent governance events. | Narrow scope is deliberate: self-improvement is high-risk enough to need a minimal profile, and the AAEL direction can add other profiles later. | `[@nist_ai_agent_standards; @eu_ai_act_article_12; @eu_ai_act_article_72]` | `schemas/asiep.schema.json`, `docs/invariants.md`, `manuscript/limitations.md` | ASIEP is not a general solution for all agents. | Define additional AAEL profiles only after ASIEP v0.1 is externally tested. |
