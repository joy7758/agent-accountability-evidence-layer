# AIES 2026 Positioning Brief for ASIEP

## Purpose

This is a planning brief only. It is not a complete paper and must not be
submitted directly. The AIES route would reposition ASIEP from FAIR evidence
infrastructure toward accountable agent-to-agent systems, governance,
institutional risk, responsibility allocation, and evidence-by-design.

## Governance Reframing

The eScience version emphasizes FAIR evidence object design, provenance,
reproducible pipeline checks, local evidence packages, and scientific
infrastructure. An AIES version would need to foreground accountability:
which actors depend on agent-produced evidence, who controls the evidence
bundle, who can challenge a promotion decision, and how responsibility is
allocated across builder agents, evaluator agents, auditors, institutions, and
human operators.

The core technical chain can remain:

```text
trace fixture -> ASIEP evidence object -> bundle resolver -> validator ->
repair plan -> package -> evaluation report -> claim/citation linting
```

But the argument must shift from infrastructure reproducibility to governance
boundaries, institutional control, agent-to-agent review, and failure modes.

## Reusable Material

- ASIEP profile definition and lifecycle.
- Validator, repairer, resolver, importer, packager, and evaluator overview.
- Claim registry and evidence map as accountability mechanisms.
- Attack corpus and local tamper-detection examples.
- Crosswalk language to PROV, OTel-like, LangSmith-like, FDO-like, and
  RO-Crate-like structures.
- Limitations about local fixture, minimal implementation, not external
  certification, no real APIs, and no production deployment.

## Additional Sociotechnical Work Needed

An AIES paper would need stronger discussion of power, institutional control,
responsibility allocation, audit rights, misuse incentives, and governance
boundaries. It would need to explain who can require an ASIEP object, who can
inspect withheld artifacts, who resolves disputed digests, and how evidence
protocols can fail when institutions control the evidence channel.

Additional sources would also be needed. M8 covers external standards and
technical related work, but an AIES submission should add social, legal, and
institutional accountability literature before final authoring.
